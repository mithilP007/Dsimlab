# SimLab Platform — Pilot Program FAQ

This FAQ answers common questions regarding simulation rules, mechanics, and workflows.

---

### Q1: What is the difference between standard simulations and daily campaigns?
*   **Standard Simulations:** Run in rounds controlled entirely by the instructor. Instructors advance the round when they decide, and students complete decisions all at once.
*   **Daily Campaigns:** Run as continuous 24-hour day steps. Students submit choices daily, and the system processes outcomes automatically at midnight (or via fast-forward sweeps during testing/rehearsals).

### Q2: What industry does the Pilot Scenario run on?
*   **Global SaaS Marketing Challenge:** The scenario industry is **B2B Software** (specifically collaborative CRM tools). The scenario runs for a maximum of **15 days** with a daily budget cap of **$2,000.00**.

### Q3: How are daily scores calculated?
Daily scores are calculated by the scoring engine across six key strategic dimensions:
1.  **Strategic Alignment (20%):** Relevancy of targeted keywords to the SaaS CRM industry.
2.  **SEO Authority & Optimization (20%):** Domain authority growth based on backlink budget and content quality.
3.  **Google Ads Efficiency (20%):** High CTR, conversions, quality score, and low CPC.
4.  **Meta Ads Efficiency (15%):** High click-through rates, ROAS, and creative quality.
5.  **Budget Discipline (10%):** Allocating spend below the scenario daily cap.
6.  **ROI/Revenue (15%):** Overall conversions and revenue generated relative to spend.

### Q4: What is the criteria for earning a signed certificate?
Students must satisfy the following thresholds upon completing the campaign:
1.  **Composite Score:** Average daily composite score must be **>= 70.0%**.
2.  **Strategic Consistency:** Transition consistency score must be **>= 65.0%** (assessing that student strategy did not change erratically day-to-day).
3.  **Instructor Approval:** For college cohorts, the instructor must review the student standings and click "Approve Certificate".

### Q5: Can I edit my campaign decisions after submitting them?
Yes, you can edit and resubmit your decisions as many times as you like, but only for the **current day**. Once a day is processed (either at midnight or via fast-forward), decisions for that day are locked and cannot be edited.

### Q6: Can multiple students work on the same campaign run?
No. Every student is provisioned their own private campaign sandbox. Other students cannot view your campaign dashboard or submit decisions on your behalf.

### Q7: How does missed-day catchup work?
If you forget to submit decisions on any day, the simulation engine will automatically execute at midnight. It will copy your decisions from the previous day, log a default decision, simulate outcomes, and advance to the next day. This ensures you do not fall behind in classroom cohorts.
